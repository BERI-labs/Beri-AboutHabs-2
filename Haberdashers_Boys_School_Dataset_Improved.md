## School Overview

Haberdashers' Boys' School (Habs Boys) is an independent day school for boys aged 4–18, located at Butterfly Lane, Elstree, Hertfordshire, WD6 3AF. Phone: 020 8266 1700. The school sits on a 100-acre co-educational campus shared with Haberdashers' Girls' School. Habs Boys was founded in 1690 by Royal Charter, funded by a bequest from Robert Aske to the Worshipful Company of Haberdashers.

The school educates around 1,450 pupils, including approximately 360 in the Prep School. The school was recently named **Independent School of the Year 2025 (overall winner)** and **Independent School of the Year for Outstanding Educational Partnerships**. It has also received fantastic ratings from *The Telegraph* and *The Good Schools Guide*, being named among the Best Value Private Schools 2025. The school motto is "Together, boundless."

## History

Founded in 1690 by Royal Charter using a bequest from Robert Aske to the Worshipful Company of Haberdashers. Originally at Hoxton in Shoreditch, the school moved to Hampstead in 1898 and to its current 100-acre site at Elstree in 1961. Aldenham House, a Grade II* listed English country house, became the school's centrepiece. Habs Boys is one of 11 schools around the country owned by the Haberdashers' Company.

In 2021, Robert Aske's name was dropped from the school following the discovery of his links with the Royal African Company and the slave trade. The motto was changed from "Serve and Obey" to "Together, boundless." The Hinton and Taylor buildings, comprising 46 classrooms, academic offices, and a drama studio, were completed in 2022.

## Pastoral Support and Wellbeing

At Habs, we understand that a boy can only reach his full academic and co-curricular potential if he feels safe, happy, and supported. Our pastoral care system is highly structured and deeply embedded into school life.

### The Buddy System

All new pupils (whether joining in Reception, Year 3, Year 7, or Year 9) are assigned a "Buddy" from an older year group. Buddies act as mentors, answering questions, helping new boys navigate the campus, and providing a friendly, familiar face during the crucial first few weeks.

### Wellbeing and PSHE

Personal, Social, Health, and Economic (PSHE) education is a core part of the curriculum. Lessons cover mental health awareness, healthy relationships, online safety, and neurodiversity. We celebrate events like Neurodiversity Week to foster understanding and inclusion.

### Medical Centre

The school has a fully staffed, on-site Medical Centre. Trained nurses are available throughout the school day to administer first aid, manage chronic conditions, and support overall physical wellbeing.

## Welcome, Induction, and Pastoral Chain

Before the academic year begins, new boys are invited to dedicated **Induction Days** designed to help them familiarise themselves with the 100-acre campus, meet their Form Tutors, and make early friendships in a relaxed environment without the pressure of a normal school day.

**Pastoral chain of communication:**

- **Form Tutors** are the primary point of contact for both students and parents. They see the boys daily and are responsible for day-to-day pastoral and academic oversight. Parents should contact the Form Tutor first with any concerns.
- **Heads of Year / Heads of House** provide secondary pastoral leadership for more significant concerns.
- **Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL):** The school has a dedicated DSL and a trained safeguarding team available at all times to handle sensitive welfare and child protection matters.

PSHCEE (Personal, Social, Health, Citizenship and Economic Education) is embedded throughout the curriculum at every year group, covering age-appropriate topics including mental health, healthy relationships, online safety, and neurodiversity.

## Scholarships — Year 7 (11+) and Year 9 (13+)

Scholarship application deadlines: **11+ entry:** Thursday 6 November 2025. **13+ entry:** Thursday 6 November 2025. VAT is charged only on the amount paid by parents (e.g. a 10% scholarship means VAT applies to the 90% paid by parents). Scholarships can be combined with bursaries. The value of a scholarship is usually between 10–15% of school fees, up to a maximum of 25% in very exceptional circumstances. The school has high expectations of scholars, who are expected to be role models who drive ambition and curiosity across the school.

**Academic Scholarships**
External candidates do not need to apply separately — following the entrance assessment, the Senior School nominates students based on academic performance. Nominated students are then invited for an interview in January, which looks for academic passion and demonstration of Habs values. Internal pupils (Year 6 for 11+ and Year 8 for 13+) are nominated based on standardised assessments and historical data including progress grades and attitude to learning. Year 8 students may also be awarded an Aske Award scholarship worth £500.

**Art Scholarships**
Candidates should demonstrate eagerness to master the craft of being a young artist. A digital portfolio (e.g. PowerPoint, max 25MB) of 6–20 pieces is required, including observational drawing, use of colour, written analysis, and brief annotations. Work in progress is welcomed. Selected candidates are invited to an interview where they bring their physical portfolio, discuss their work, and undertake a short practical task.

**Drama Scholarships**
The school looks for potential, good natural instincts, a collaborative spirit, and a passion for storytelling. Formal qualifications are not an advantage. External students submit an application form and a drama teacher reference. Selected students are invited for a workshop audition in January.

**Design and Technology Scholarships (13+ only)**
Candidates should demonstrate the ability to break down complex problems and an interest in taking a leading role in the D&T department. A digital portfolio is required showing products made (in woods, metals, plastics), creative sketches, and written accounts. Assessment includes an interview and a design problem-based question.

**Music Scholarships**
The school looks for exceptional potential and innate musicianship. Candidates normally offer two instruments: principal instrument at approximately Grade 5 standard (Grade 6/7 if piano), and a second instrument at approximately Grade 3. Actual grade certificates are not essential if playing at this level. Awards include Scholarships (10–15% fee reduction) or Exhibitions (free tuition on one instrument). The audition involves performing a piece, brief musicianship tests, sight reading, and a short interview.

**Sports Scholarships**
Candidates should demonstrate sustained sporting excellence and representative experience at county level or above, with particular encouragement for those excelling in football, hockey, and cricket. Benefits include access to the High-Performance Programme, 1:1 mentoring, Mind Health Programme, Sports Nutrition seminars, and bespoke Strength and Conditioning profiling. Assessment includes a practical session (sport aptitude test, fitness testing, main sport activities) and an interview.

## Curriculum: Senior School Lower (Years 7–8, Ages 11–13)

Boys joining in Year 7 enter a structured but exploratory phase of their education. The curriculum is broad by design — students are expected to engage across English, Mathematics, separately taught Sciences (Biology, Chemistry, Physics), Humanities, Computing, Art, Music, Drama, PE, and Modern Foreign Languages. The school is not bound by the National Curriculum but stays current with national developments and exceeds them in depth and academic stretch.

**Languages:** In Year 7, boys experience a carousel of French, Spanish, and German before choosing two languages to continue from Year 8 onwards.

**Academic enrichment:** The **Family History Project** in Year 7 is a structured independent research task that introduces extended academic writing and source analysis. The **Science Fair** runs across Years 7 and 8, encouraging experimental thinking and presentation skills. Boys also participate in national and international competitions including Maths and Science Olympiads, and engage with external speakers and academic societies from early in their time at the school.

Sets are used but are flexible and reviewed regularly, ensuring boys are always working in the most appropriate group for their current level.

The school operates a **no-phone policy** throughout Years 7–11. All Senior School students have lockers and are expected to take pride in their organisation and presentation.

## Curriculum: Senior School Upper / GCSE (Years 9–11, Ages 13–16)

**Year 9** serves as a deliberate bridge year, introducing more advanced concepts and greater academic independence ahead of the formal GCSE programme. The **Dawson Independent Research Project** is undertaken in Year 9, requiring students to pursue an extended piece of academic research in a subject of their choosing — developing skills of scholarship that underpin both GCSE and Sixth Form study.

**Years 10 and 11** are focused entirely on preparation for GCSE examinations. ## Diversity and Inclusion

Habs is proud of the huge range of cultures, backgrounds, identities, and beliefs represented in its community. This diversity is both welcoming for students and an invaluable preparation for life in a diverse society and globalised world.

## ISI Inspection Reports

Haberdashers' Boys' School was inspected on 11–13 February 2025 by the Independent Schools Inspectorate (ISI). Under the current ISI framework, the reports assess schools against five key standards as "met" or "not met," and evaluate pupil experience across four core areas (Leadership, management and governance; Quality of education, training and recreation; Pupils' physical and mental health and emotional wellbeing; Pupils' social and economic education and contribution to society) alongside Safeguarding.

**Result:** All inspection standards were met. The school was awarded **two significant strengths** — the highest accolade in the new ISI framework:

1. The co-curricular programme was recognised as a significant strength, providing significant variety with many activities initiated or organised by pupils. Although voluntary, all pupils take part, promoting pupils' wellbeing and providing balance to their examination studies.
2. Prep school leaders' revision of the English curriculum was recognised as a significant strength, with pupils' high levels of attainment in literacy in this phase of education.

Previous inspections: ISI 2022 (Senior School and Prep School reports available).

## Old Haberdashers (Alumni Network)

Alumni of Habs Boys and Habs Girls are known as "Old Haberdashers." The bonds formed as students last a lifetime — it is not unusual for Old Haberdashers to return for a 50-year reunion in the company of close friends made in class, or with a spouse met on the school coach.

Old Haberdashers are part of a wide network of fellow alumni of all ages and from all walks of life. Many find mentors, future colleagues, and friends through the Old Haberdasher network.
